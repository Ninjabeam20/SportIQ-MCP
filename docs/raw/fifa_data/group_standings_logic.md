# FIFA 2026 World Cup Group Standings Logic

This document outlines the official rules used to determine the rankings of teams within the group stage of the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

## 1. Primary Ranking Criteria
The ranking of each team in each group is determined sequentially by:
1. **Points:** Greatest number of points obtained in all group matches (3 points for a win, 1 point for a draw, 0 points for a loss).
2. **Goal Difference:** Goal difference in all group matches (Total Goals Scored minus Total Goals Conceded).
3. **Goals Scored:** Greatest number of goals scored in all group matches.

## 2. Tiebreaker Criteria
If two or more teams in the same group are equal on the basis of the three primary criteria above, their rankings will be determined by the following tiebreaker rules in order:

4. **Head-to-Head Points:** Greatest number of points obtained in the group matches between the teams concerned.
5. **Head-to-Head Goal Difference:** Goal difference resulting from the group matches between the teams concerned.
6. **Head-to-Head Goals Scored:** Greatest number of goals scored in the group matches between the teams concerned.
7. **Fair Play Conduct:** The team with the highest fair play score (fewest deduction points) based on yellow and red cards received by players/team officials in all group matches:
   - **Yellow Card:** -1 point
   - **Indirect Red Card (second yellow card):** -3 points
   - **Direct Red Card:** -4 points
   - **Yellow Card and Direct Red Card:** -5 points
   *Note: Only one of the above deductions is applied to a single player in a single match.*
8. **Drawing of Lots:** Drawing of lots by the FIFA Organizing Committee.
