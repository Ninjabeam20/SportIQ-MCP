# Tournament Monte Carlo (flagship engine)

Drives `football_simulate_bracket`. Per iteration: simulate all **12 groups**, take the **top 2 + 8 best third-placed** teams (32 qualifiers — the 2026 format), slot them into the **official FIFA 2026 knockout structure**, and play it to a champion. Aggregating gives each team's probability of reaching every round and winning.

## Knockout
- Ties sampled from [[poisson-xg]]; draws after normal time go to a shootout weighted by [[elo]] `expected_score`.
- Qualifiers fill the official **R32 slot template** (matches 73–88). The 8 best thirds are mapped to winner slots via the **Annex C allocation table** (495 combinations, keyed by which group letters finish third), and the 32 teams are ordered by `bracket_order` so adjacent pairs feed the fixed R16→Final tree.

## Official seeding data
The knockout structure lives in `src/sportiq/football/data/wc2026_bracket.json`, generated by `scripts/build_wc2026_bracket.py` from the validated CSVs in `docs/raw/fifa_data/` (R32 template, `bracket_order`, and the 495-row best-thirds allocation). The build script asserts the table is complete (C(12,8)=495) and bijective (no slot faces a third from its own group). This replaced the earlier strength-seed stand-in (C1). See [[0008-football-fallback-strategy]].

## Invariants (tested)
- `reach_r32` mass across teams == 32 (exactly 32 qualify each iteration).
- `win` mass == 1 (one champion per iteration).
- Round probabilities are monotone; champion title-probability is stable (±2%) across seeds at ~10k iterations.
